⚠️ Counterintuitive Finding on Deprivation

  • UnexpectedLeast deprived areas (bands 9–10) show the HIGHEST average DNA rate at 7.40% — not the most deprived (6.12%).
  • CaveatOnly 4 areas in the least deprived band vs 7 in bands 5–6. Wide min–max ranges (3.30%–11.30%) suggest outliers dominate the average.
  • InferenceDeprivation alone does not reliably predict DNA rate here — urban density, appointment mode mix and access patterns are likely confounders.

📊 Distribution of Sub-ICB DNA Rates — how are they spread?

🔑 1 · London is a national outlier demanding targeted action

  • 12.15%London's DNA rate is 2.7× higher than South West (4.51%) and 40% worse than the next worst region (North West, 11.84%).
  • ImplicationUrban density, transient population and high face-to-face volume likely drive this. Targeted London interventions could have the biggest national impact per pound spent.

🔑 2 · Appointment mode is the single strongest lever available

  • 5.5× gapFace-to-face DNA rate (10.65%) is 5.5× higher than video/online (1.94%) and 1.8× higher than telephone (5.77%).
  • OpportunityEvery 1% shift from face-to-face to telephone or digital saves thousands of appointments nationally. The mechanism is intuitive: lower friction = lower no-show, like how a phone call to your bank feels less daunting than walking in.
  • TrendVideo/online has grown from 0.58% → 5.54% of all appointments (Jul 22 – Dec 24), a near 10× increase. Momentum exists but ceiling is far higher.

🔑 3 · Stable national average masks dramatic local variation

  • 4.4–5.9%National rates fluctuate in a narrow band — no systemic national crisis. But this is like a national average temperature hiding a heatwave in one city and snow in another.
  • 32.84%Staffs & Stoke-on-Trent (05Q) has a rate 15× higher than the 0% bottom performers. Even excluding that outlier, the range is ~3%–22%.
  • ImplicationPolicy must be locally targeted, not nationally uniform. Best performers have clear lessons for the worst.

🔑 4 · Deprivation relationship is weaker and more complex than assumed

  • CounterintuitiveLeast deprived areas (bands 9–10) average 7.40% DNA vs 6.12% for more deprived bands (5–6) — the opposite of the expected pattern.
  • CaveatSmall counts per band and huge ranges suggest confounders (urban mix, F2F vs telephone volume) dominate over deprivation signal.
  • ImplicationDo not assume deprived areas are the primary DNA problem. Both ends of the deprivation spectrum contain high-DNA sub-ICBs requiring investigation.

🔑 5 · North West is a secondary hotspot beyond London

  • 11.84%North West is second highest region, and 5 of the top 10 worst sub-ICBs sit within it (Greater Manchester, Lancashire, Cheshire clusters).
  • ImplicationThe concentration within North West suggests systemic, regional issues — not isolated GP-level problems. A regional improvement programme is warranted.
